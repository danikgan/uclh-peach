Research

Given that our project has been divided into several sub-tasks, our team has had to explore a wide array of technologies and tools.

One of the biggest worries of the client was to ensure the compatibility of the separate teamsâ€™ work on the project. To this end, following discussions on Ryver between the separate teams, it was discovered that most teams were comfortable with either using Angular2.JS for development, or ReactJS. Consequently, as the central UI team, it fell upon our team to make the final decision. Therefore we had to investigate the advantages and disadvantages of developing with either language.

On the theme of integration, our client proposed we use Electrode to enforce standardisation of development. We therefore needed to investigate the viability of incorporating Electrode and its ease of integrating it into development.

With regard to the Design Guide website, the client wished for it to be responsive so that it would be viewable from any device. The client helpfully pointed out the framework Bootstrap. Although it would appear an obvious choice, given that it enjoys one of the largest developer communities, alongside all the support that entails, we investigated alternative frameworks such as Material UI and Pure.CSS. We also had to research the viability of using either the tried and tested Bootstrap 3, or the newer Bootstrap 4.

For the Dashboards project itself, our primary focus was finding a language that would aid in data visualisation. To this end, we first investigated D3.js, the most popular data visualisation tool. We also investigated several competitors including Google Charts, ChartJS and N3-charts.

Something that would primarily be of use for the Dashboard site, given that it is regularly updated with new data, but also for the Design Guide site, would be the incorporation of a static site generator. To this end we had to investigate how static site generators work and the alternatives to static site generators. We then investigated the use of Middleman, and to an extent Jekyll, as these seemed to be the most popular static site generators currently available.
