Our Project


The Peach Dashboards project is highly multifaceted in nature. As the central User Interface team within the PEACH project, our role constantly evolves and changes to take into account the work done by, and requirements of, other teams.


Context:

The PEACH project is an ambitious project that aims to ‘provide a data science tool for use by medical professionals and researchers to aid diagnostic and analytics processes through the use of big data, machine learning and data visualisation’ (https://code4health.org/peach, 2016). Within this project, our client is focusing primarily on data collection and sharing with regard to cancer. Our team is tasked primarily with the front end user interface, particularly with respect to the visualisation of the this data collected. This is to be done through the ‘Dashboards’. However our responsibilities are spread over several sub-projects related to these dashboards.


What do these sub-projects do?

The PEACH Dashboards are a means of visually presenting patient data taken from UCLH in an easily readable way, that will be able to be readily accessed by the different specialists involved in the treatment of a cancer patient, allowing greater integration of patient care.


Visual Report:

The team has also been given the opportunity provide the finishing touches to the Visual Report software, built by Msc Computer Science students at UCL in the summer of 2016. This software will allow Radiologists to report prostate MRIs, via ‘painting’ on the screen and add text, allowing this critical information to be shared more readily with other oncology exports.


Design Guide:

Reflecting our central role as the UI team, we have also been placed responsible for the continued development of the UCLH Peach design guide website. It is here that all other PEACH teams and future teams will find design rules for developing new PEACH websites, ensuring consistency across the project.


Form Renderer:

The team has also been assigned responsibility for developing the front end component of the form renderer, which is the means that future users of the dashboards will input user data.


The Client:

Our client is Dr. Navin Ramachandran, a consultant based at UCLH. He is a highly respected Radiologist, and a well known name within the medical community. As a result of the problems he encountered with regard to clinical data collection and sharing within the field of oncology, he has been a key figure in establishing the PEACH project.


Challenges:

Our client, at the beginning of the project, made us acutely aware of the fact that the failure of the previous developers (Msc Computer Science students) to produce a successful dashboard feature was not down to any inadequacy with regard to technical knowledge, but rather down to an inability to successfully communicate with other teams. This ultimately lead to the separate sub-projects becoming incompatible with each other, and thereby could not be integrated within the larger project. The previous team also left very little documentation, therefore it is near impossible to salvage, or reuse what code they had written.


What we need to succeed:

Given the lessons learned by previous teams, it is of critical importance that our team remain in constant discussion with all others, particularly with regard to development frameworks and languages. Given the team’s broad remit, it will be of vital importance to secure client requirements at an early stage, as the potential for ‘scope creep’ is huge. It is also of vital importance that all work is documented thoroughly, so as to aid future development on the project.
